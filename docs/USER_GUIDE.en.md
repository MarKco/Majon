# Majon — User Guide 🧶

Majon counts the rows of your knitting projects, reminds you of special
instructions at exactly the right moment, and shows how far you are from
the finish line.

> "Needles done" and "rows" mean the same here: every time you finish a row,
> tap **+**.

## 1. Create a project

1. From the home screen tap **New project**.
2. Give it a name (e.g. "Red sweater").
3. Pick an **icon** (sweater, scarf, hat, socks, gloves, blanket, bag, yarn
   ball) and a **color** — handy when you knit several similar things at
   once (three scarves? three colors).
4. Optional: note the **yarn** (e.g. "100% merino wool") and the **needles**
   (e.g. "4.5"). Future-you will be grateful when re-knitting it.
5. **Save**.

To edit or delete a project: **⋮** menu on its card, or the top-right menu
inside the project.

## 2. Add the parts

A project splits into parts: front, back, sleeves, collar…

1. Open the project and tap **Add part**.
2. Enter a name (e.g. "Front") and the expected **total rows** (e.g. 120).

Total rows drive the progress percentage: the bar at the top shows the
whole project, each part shows its own.

> If you lower the total below the rows already done, the app warns you.

## 3. Count rows

Tap a part to open the **counter**:

- The big number is the row **you are knitting right now**.
- **+** (big button): row done. The counter advances with a gentle
  confirmation vibration.
- **−**: undo the last row (for accidental taps).
- Top **⋮** menu: **Go to row…** to jump to an exact row, **Reset counter**
  to start over (notes are kept), **Notes** to manage instructions.
- When the part is finished: 🎉.

The screen stays on while the counter is open (can be turned off in
Settings).

## 4. Notes — instructions at the right moment

Pattern instructions like "decrease by two at row 19" become **notes**: they
pop up by themselves, highlighted, **when you are about to knit that row** —
plus a preview one row earlier ("Next: row 19 · decrease by two").

From the counter: **⋮ → Notes → Add note**.

A note has:
- **Text**: the instruction ("decrease by two", "switch color"…)
- **From row / To row**: a single row (19–19) or a range (20–30)
- **Applies to**: every row, **odd** rows only, or **even** rows only

Real-life examples:

| Pattern says | How you enter it |
|---|---|
| "At row 19 decrease by 2" | rows 19–19, every row |
| "From row 20 to 25 decrease by 2" | rows 20–25, every row |
| "Odd rows in stockinette" | rows 1–120, odd rows |
| "Odd rows from 20 to 30: 2 yarn-overs" | rows 20–30, odd rows |

Enter all the pattern's instructions when you start: from then on, your only
job is tapping **+**.

## 5. Settings ⚙️

- **Language**: System / Italiano / English — switches instantly.
- **Theme**: System / Light / Dark.
- **Dynamic colors**: use your phone's wallpaper colors (Android 12+)
  instead of Majon's palette.
- **Vibration**: haptic feedback on **+**.
- **Keep screen on**: while the counter is open.

## 6. Back up your data 💾

Your data lives only on your phone. To keep it safe:

- **Export data**: saves everything (projects, parts, counts, notes) to a
  JSON file wherever you like (Drive, Downloads…).
- **Import data**: loads a backup. Imported projects are **added** to the
  existing ones — nothing gets overwritten.

Useful when switching phones, or as a safety net.

## FAQ

**I tapped + by mistake.** Tap **−**.

**I lost count.** Count the rows on your work, then **⋮ → Go to row…**

**Can I knit several projects at once?** Yes, no limits: every project and
part keeps its own count.

**Does resetting the counter delete my notes?** No, notes are kept.

**Does the app need permissions?** None. No internet, no ads: your data
stays yours.
